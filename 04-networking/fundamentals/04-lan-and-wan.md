# LAN And WAN

## Overview

This section introduces the different types of **computer networks**, focusing on **Local Area Networks (LAN)** and **Wide Area Networks (WAN)**. It explains how networks are structured based on size, scope, and how devices are connected.

This is useful in real-world environments where understanding the difference between **local networks** and **global networks** is essential. These concepts form the foundation for how systems communicate across **infrastructure** and the **internet**.

---

## Commands

This section is concept-focused, but these tools will later be used to apply your knowledge:

- `ip a` → View **network interfaces and IP addresses**  
- `ping` → Test **connectivity within a network**  
- `traceroute` → Trace how traffic moves across **networks**  
- `netstat` / `ss` → Inspect **network connections**  

---

## Key Takeaways

- **LAN (Local Area Network)** connects devices within a small area (home, office)  
- **WAN (Wide Area Network)** connects multiple LANs across larger regions  
- LANs are typically faster and locally managed, while WANs span wider distances  
- The **internet** is the largest example of a WAN  
- Understanding network types is key for designing and troubleshooting systems
